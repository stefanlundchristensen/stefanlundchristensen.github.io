## 1. Enabling-to-Product Org Change

**Post Theme:** Transitioning platform teams from enablement-focused to product-focused structure

### Recommended Articles:

#### Supporting Your Perspective:

1. **[What is Platform as a Product? Clues from Team Topologies](https://teamtopologies.com/videos-slides/what-is-platform-as-a-product-clues-from-team-topologies)** - Team Topologies
   - **Where it helps:** Section on "Platform as Product" reframing. Manuel Pais explains how platforms offer curated experiences to internal "customers," which aligns with your shift from "tell teams how" to "give teams tooling that does the right thing by default."
   - **Specific value:** Can strengthen your argument about platform teams needing to think like product teams with user research and dialog.

2. **[Mind the Platform Execution Gap](https://martinfowler.com/articles/platform-prerequisites.html)** - Evan Bottcher, ThoughtWorks
   - **Where it helps:** Your "Why the Old Structure Didn't Work" section. Bottcher's definition of platforms as "self-service APIs, tools, services, knowledge and support, arranged as a compelling internal product" reinforces your product-focused approach.
   - **Specific value:** The emphasis on "compelling internal product" validates your shift from abstract enablement to concrete products.

3. **[Platform Engineering in 2026: 5 Shifts Driving the Rise](https://www.growin.com/blog/platform-engineering-2026/)** - Growin
   - **Where it helps:** Your section on measuring success and outcomes. The article discusses how 80% of large software organizations will have platform teams by 2026, with emphasis on measuring ROI in business terms.
   - **Specific value:** Supports your argument about visibility of impact and concrete outcomes over abstract enablement.

4. **[10 Platform Engineering Predictions for 2026](https://platformengineering.org/blog/10-platform-engineering-predictions-for-2026)** - Platform Engineering Community
   - **Where it helps:** Your "What Changed Structurally" section and the product-focused leader mandate.
   - **Specific value:** Discussion of how successful platforms communicate ROI in business terms aligns with your outcome-focused transformation.

5. **[Platform Engineering's 2026 Reckoning: Shift Down, AI Fusion](https://www.webpronews.com/platform-engineerings-2026-reckoning-shift-down-ai-fusion-and-the-80-mandate/)** - WebProNews
   - **Where it helps:** Your discussion of "saying no" and having a point of view. The "shift down" principle advocates for embedding decisions into platforms rather than offering maximum optionality.
   - **Specific value:** Reinforces that being product-focused means making opinionated choices, not providing endless options.

#### Challenging/Alternative Perspective:

6. **[The Big Product and Platform Shift](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/tech-and-ai/our-insights/the-big-product-and-platform-shift-five-actions-to-get-the-transformation-right)** - McKinsey
   - **Where it adds nuance:** While supporting platform-as-product, McKinsey emphasizes five actions including "reimagining the tech operating model" which is more complex than just reframing.
   - **Specific value:** Could add depth to your "Managing the Transition" section by acknowledging this is part of a larger operating model transformation, not just a reframing exercise.

## 2. European Payment Rails

**Post Theme:** Navigating European payment infrastructure complexity and fragmentation

### Recommended Articles:

#### Supporting Your Perspective:

1. **[How to Implement SEPA Instant Payments Ahead of the Deadlines](https://dashdevs.com/blog/sepa-instant-payments/)** - DashDevs
   - **Where it helps:** Your section on A2A payments and SEPA Instant. Explains the mandatory January 2025 implementation and technical challenges.
   - **Specific value:** Adds urgency and technical depth to your discussion of regulatory changes and infrastructure investments.

2. **[European Payment Landscape 2025: Data-Driven Report](https://www.tembi.io/blog/payment-providers-in-european-e-commerce-a-country-by-country-analysis)** - Tembi
   - **Where it helps:** Your "Card Payments" section discussing Girocard, Cartes Bancaires, and Bancontact market dominance.
   - **Specific value:** Provides recent market share data to validate your claims about local scheme importance.

3. **[EU Instant Payments: Challenges and Compliance by 2025](https://www.ey.com/en_gl/insights/financial-services/emeia/eu-instant-payments-regulation-five-key-hurdles-for-banks-to-clear)** - EY
   - **Where it helps:** Your "Regulatory Navigation" section. EY identifies five key hurdles including AML checks within 10 seconds, which relates to your point about different regulatory interpretations.
   - **Specific value:** Validates your experience that "the rule was identical, the regulator's interpretation wasn't."

4. **[From PSD2 to PSD3: What's Changing in Europe](https://www.trustbuilder.com/en/psd2-psd3-directive-future-payments-europe/)** - TrustBuilder
   - **Where it helps:** Your mention of PSD3 coming and shifting the landscape. This article explains PSD3's broader scope including BNPL, cryptocurrencies, and standardized APIs.
   - **Specific value:** Could expand your "Monitor regulatory changes" recommendation with specific PSD3 implications.

5. **[Payment Methods in Europe: A 2025 Guide for E-Commerce](https://fueler.io/blog/payment-methods-in-europe-a-guide-for-ecommerce-businesses)** - Fueler
   - **Where it helps:** Your entire premise about fragmentation. Discusses how credit/debit cards, digital wallets, local bank transfers, and BNPL are most widely used, but local preferences vary significantly.
   - **Specific value:** Reinforces your conversion drop example with Germans expecting Girocard.

#### Challenging/Alternative Perspective:

6. **[Worldpay Global Payments Report 2025](https://www.worldpay.com/en/global-payments-report)** - Worldpay/FIS
   - **Where it adds nuance:** While confirming fragmentation, shows convergence trends - digital wallets and A2A payments gaining share, with cards projected to drop to 33% by 2026.
   - **Specific value:** Could add forward-looking perspective to your post: fragmentation is real now, but the landscape is consolidating around digital methods, which might change your "building for scale" recommendations.

## 3. Goal Setting

**Post Theme:** Effective goal-setting without new frameworks, based on Amazon's 6-pager approach with additions

### Recommended Articles:

#### Supporting Your Perspective:

1. **[TBM 115/2: Healthy Goals](https://cutlefish.substack.com/p/tbm-1152-healthy-goals)** - John Cutler
   - **Where it helps:** Your section on psychological safety and confidence updates. Cutler discusses why the "why" behind goals matters more than the framework.
   - **Specific value:** Validates your point that "low confidence should trigger help, not blame" and your emphasis on psychological safety in goal reviews.

2. **[TBM 368: Goals Take Practice](https://cutlefish.substack.com/p/tbm-368-goals-take-practice)** - John Cutler
   - **Where it helps:** Your criticism of framework proliferation. Cutler argues goal-setting is a continuous skill requiring practice, not a one-time framework adoption.
   - **Specific value:** Reinforces your message: "You don't need another framework. You need clarity."

3. **[7 Goal-Setting Frameworks: Alternatives to OKRs](https://www.tability.io/odt/articles/7-goal-setting-framework-alternatives-to-okrs)** - Tability
   - **Where it helps:** Your opening about OKR workshops and framework fatigue. Lists SMART, BHAGs, MBOs, V2MOMs as alternatives, which you reference.
   - **Specific value:** Validates that there are indeed "a new framework that promises to be the answer" each year, supporting your critique of framework proliferation.

4. **[A Proven OKR Alternative: The WhyGo Framework](https://scotchisholm.com/okr-alternative-whygo-goal-setting-system/)** - Scot Chisholm
   - **Where it helps:** Your point about OKRs creating corporate theater. Chisholm explains how OKRs "provide too much flexibility and too little guidance" and break down around 25-50 people.
   - **Specific value:** Validates your critique that frameworks become too complex and lose practical value at scale.

5. **[Radical Focus: Accomplish Big Goals with OKRs](https://www.lennysnewsletter.com/p/the-ultimate-guide-to-okrs-christina)** - Christina Wodtke
   - **Where it helps:** Your emphasis on focus. Wodtke's "Radical Focus" title emphasizes focus as the most critical factor, not the framework itself.
   - **Specific value:** While she uses OKRs, her emphasis on radical focus over framework perfection aligns with your pragmatic approach.

#### Challenging/Alternative Perspective:

6. **[A Flight-Tested Framework for Effective Goal-Setting](https://mixpanel.com/blog/a-flight-tested-framework-for-effective-goal-setting/)** - Lenny Rachitsky (via Mixpanel)
   - **Where it adds nuance:** Lenny's framework does include structure (Situation, Complication, Resolution with 3-5 strategic pillars), suggesting some framework can be helpful.
   - **Specific value:** Could acknowledge that while you don't need a new framework, having *some* structure (like Amazon's 6-pager pattern) is valuable. Your post actually proposes a framework; the distinction is it's simple and focused.

2. **[bliki: Conway's Law](https://martinfowler.com/bliki/ConwaysLaw.html)** - Martin Fowler
   - **Where it helps:** The "Inverse Conway Maneuver" concept supports your deliberate organizational design.

   - **Where it helps:** Your principle about "Clear boundaries" and "Decision-Making Authority." Cagan discusses balancing team autonomy with organizational mission.
   - **Specific value:** Adds nuance that autonomy requires context-setting from leadership, which you address in your "strong context-setting" point.

4. **[Transitioning to Autonomous Product Teams](https://medium.com/technology-earnin/transitioning-to-autonomous-product-teams-8260ed9d79dd)** - Jessica Chen Riolfi, Earnin
   - **Where it helps:** Your entire post about the transition from 100 to 850 people. Real-world example of the transition.
   - **Specific value:** Validates that this model is achievable and provides comparison point for your Pleo experience.

5. **[Decentralized Leadership: Organizational Agility & Innovation](https://ceonetweavers.org/decentralized-leadership/)** - CEON Foundation
   - **Where it helps:** Your discussion of decision-making authority and trust in good intent.
   - **Specific value:** Broader framework for understanding decentralization benefits beyond just speed.

#### Challenging/Alternative Perspective:

6. **[Finding the Sweet Spot for Organizational Control and Team Autonomy](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10664-021-09967-3)** - Empirical Software Engineering Journal
   - **Where it adds nuance:** Academic research showing there's a tension between control and autonomy that needs balancing, not just maximizing autonomy.
   - **Specific value:** Could acknowledge that complete self-sufficiency has coordination costs. Your model at Pleo had "clear boundaries between units" and "strong context-setting" as necessary guardrails - full autonomy without these doesn't work.

   - **Where it helps:** Your discussion of how meeting framing affects team behavior. Research shows stand-ups positively relate to psychological safety.
   - **Specific value:** Empirical backing that meeting structure and facilitation affect team safety and performance.

4. **[Radical Candor: Feedback Training & Coaching](https://www.radicalcandor.com/our-approach)** - Kim Scott
   - **Where it helps:** Your section on "Making It Real" - asking good questions and not using meetings as surveillance.

   - **Specific value:** Could acknowledge that renaming meetings is necessary but insufficient - if senior leadership doesn't model vulnerability and support, middle managers still won't feel safe surfacing problems in any type of meeting.

## Summary of Perspectives:

### What You'll Find Across These Articles:

**Supporting Evidence:**
- Platform-as-product is industry best practice (Team Topologies, Evan Bottcher, Platform Engineering community)
- European payments fragmentation is real and challenging (Worldpay, EY, fintech infrastructure providers)
- Goal-setting frameworks proliferate but simple clarity wins (John Cutler, Scot Chisholm)
- Leadership at scale requires different dimensions than team leadership (Will Larson, Camille Fournier)
- Payment systems need careful architecture and modularity (Stripe, FinLego, industry reports)
- Self-sufficient teams aligned with Conway's Law work at scale (Marty Cagan, Martin Fowler)
- Meeting framing affects psychological safety (academic research, Kim Scott, Patrick Lencioni)

**Challenging Perspectives:**
- Platform-as-product is part of larger operating model transformation, not just reframing (McKinsey)
- European payments fragmenting but also consolidating around digital methods (Worldpay trends)
- Some structure/framework can be helpful even if you critique framework proliferation (Lenny Rachitsky)
- Leadership requires influencing systems beyond your control, not just your five dimensions (Patrick Kua)
- Mature platforms (Stripe) do provide reliable abstractions - knowing when to trust matters (Stripe blog)
- Team Topologies oversimplifies; single approach to complexity has limitations (Patricia Aas)
- Full autonomy needs balancing with coordination; sweet spot exists (academic research)
- Renaming meetings insufficient if senior leadership doesn't model vulnerability (HBR on middle managers)

## How to Use These Recommendations:

1. **Read articles that challenge your perspective first** - They'll help you strengthen your arguments by addressing potential objections
2. **Look for specific examples and data** - These articles provide market data, research, and concrete examples you can reference
3. **Consider adding "Further Reading" sections** - Like you did in the goal-setting post, curate 3-5 key articles for readers
4. **Use quotes selectively** - Citing recognized authorities (Larson, Fournier, Cagan, Cutler) adds credibility
5. **Acknowledge limitations** - Where challenging perspectives reveal tradeoffs, briefly acknowledging them makes your posts more intellectually honest
